# HOPLINE

A tiny blocky critter trying to cross an endless run of roads, rivers and train tracks without getting squished. That is the whole game.

## the story

I saw people on Twitter shipping games for the Orynth game cup and figured I would have a go at it too. I had never built a game before this one. I mostly wanted to mess around with an idea and see how far I could take it before the deadline.

It started as "what if you just hop across a road" and grew a little every time I sat down with it. I would add one small thing, come back the next day and add another, and at some point it turned into something I actually enjoyed playing. So here it is, my attempt at the cup.

## what it is

You play a small voxel creature hopping forward as far as you can. The roads are full of cars and trucks, the rivers make you ride across moving logs, and the train tracks have trains that do not care about you at all. Sit still too long and an eagle turns up to carry you off. Collect coins, keep a combo going, squeeze past traffic, and try to beat your last run.

## how to play

- Arrow keys or **WASD** to hop around
- **Esc** to pause
- Keep moving forward, because the eagle gets bored of waiting fast

You will pick it up in a few seconds. There is nothing to read first.

## what ended up in it

I kept tinkering, so a few extra layers crept in:

- **Biomes** that change as you go: sunny isles, neon city, autumn woods, frostpeak. The first one lasts a good while so the switch feels like you earned it.
- **Coins** that tend to sit right where it is risky to grab them, which is kind of the point.
- A **combo** that builds while you keep moving forward and drops if you stall or go backwards.
- **Near misses** that briefly slow time when a car or a train scrapes past you.
- **Characters you unlock** with your coins, and each one has an actual ability like a coin magnet, doubled coins, an extra life, or never drowning.
- A **day and night cycle** that shifts the sky from bright afternoon through to neon dark.
- **Three chiptune loops** generated in code with no audio files, one picked at random per run.
- Music and sound effects have their own separate mute toggles.

Your score is your distance plus coins and near misses, multiplied by your combo. The braver you play, the more you make.

## run it

Download the html file and double click it. There is nothing to install. It opens in any modern browser as a single file. It loads three.js from a cdn and I built the rest by hand.
